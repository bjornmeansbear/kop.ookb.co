A Kit of Parts

A Design Workshop. April 8 – May 6, 2013

An Explanation

Until recently the “design” of everyday things as we know it wasn’t just about creative individuals coming up with new, unique solutions. It was much more about seeing what worked around you and adopting it to your needs. Different places evolved different solutions based on their constraints of material, weather, needs, social beliefs, etc. This created various design “vernaculars” — design patterns and solutions that were used by everyone around. Craftspeople would actually trade pattern books, something that told you how to build or create specific looks, and what materials and methods were required to do those things. These were basically rules for making.

As designers, we are often given a seemingly limited set of colors, images and text by a client — clutter in the form of assets — and asked to assign meaning and visual organization, thereby creating design.

In this project we will start by exploring how a random assortment of visual matter (clutter) can become charged, poetic and strange by combining type, images and colors and making intuitive compositions. Each week we will add a constraint, allowing for the evolution and growth of a simple visual system, which also communicates a message.
